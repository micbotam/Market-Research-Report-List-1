<p><p>The pertussis vaccination market is primarily divided into two types: acellular vaccines and whole-cell vaccines. Acellular vaccines, which contain purified components of the Bordetella pertussis bacterium, offer reduced side effects and are favored for their safety profile. Whole-cell vaccines, made from killed bacteria, provide a robust immune response but are associated with more side effects. As public health initiatives focus on improving vaccine uptake and safety, the acellular vaccine market has gained significant traction in recent years.</p></p>
