<p><p>Membrane separation technologies encompass various filtration methods used to separate substances in liquids or gases. Reverse osmosis (RO) employs a semipermeable membrane to remove ions and small molecules. Ultrafiltration (UF) targets larger particles and colloids, while microfiltration (MF) filters out even larger particles such as bacteria. Nanofiltration (NF) bridges the gap between UF and RO, selectively removing divalent ions and organic molecules. Other technologies may include electrofiltration and membrane distillation, catering to diverse industrial and environmental applications.</p></p>
