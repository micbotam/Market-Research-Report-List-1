<p><ul><li>Double Beam Microscope</li><li>Multibeam Microscope</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Interference microscopes utilize the principles of light interference to enhance imaging resolution and contrast. The double beam microscope employs two beams of light, helping to extract detailed information from samples by comparing the light transmitted through a specimen with a reference beam. In contrast, multibeam microscopes utilize multiple beams simultaneously, enabling quicker data acquisition and improved imaging of large or complex samples. Both types are essential in fields like materials science and biology for precise measurements and analysis.</p></p>
