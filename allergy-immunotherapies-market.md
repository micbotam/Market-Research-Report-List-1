<p><p>Allergy immunotherapies are treatments aimed at desensitizing patients to allergens. Subcutaneous Immunotherapy (SCIT) involves administering allergen injections beneath the skin, gradually increasing dosage to build tolerance over time. This method is effective for a wide range of allergies but requires regular visits to a healthcare provider. Conversely, Sublingual Immunotherapy (SLIT) involves placing allergen tablets or drops under the tongue, allowing for at-home administration. SLIT is preferred for its convenience and safety, making it an attractive option for many patients.</p></p>
