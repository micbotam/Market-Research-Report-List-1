<p><p>Ambulatory Surgical Centres (ASCs) are classified into two main types: hospital-based and freestanding. Hospital-based ASCs are integrated within a hospital, providing surgical services while benefiting from institutional resources and support. They often cater to higher-acuity cases due to their access to hospital facilities and specialized staff. In contrast, freestanding ASCs operate independently from hospitals, offering outpatient surgical services in a dedicated facility. They typically focus on lower-risk procedures, providing a more streamlined and cost-effective option for patients.</p></p>
