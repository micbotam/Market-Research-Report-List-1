<p><p>The Bismuth Germanate Scintillation Crystal market is primarily influenced by two growth methods: the Czochralski Method and the Bridgman Method. The Czochralski Method involves melting the materials and gradually pulling a crystal from the melt, offering high purity and uniformity, ideal for high-performance applications. In contrast, the Bridgman Method involves the controlled cooling of molten material in a vertical furnace, typically resulting in larger crystal sizes but with more orientation defects. Both methods cater to diverse applications in radiation detection and imaging.</p></p>
